# Glossary

A quick reference for the terms, concepts, and components used throughout tAhIni’s documentation. This helps instructors, developers, and administrators stay on the same page—literally and technically.

## Assessment

Any graded or ungraded task assigned to a student, including exams, quizzes, assignments, or structured responses.

## Assessment Integrity

The degree to which an exam or assessment can be trusted to reflect a student’s own work without unfair assistance or tampering.

## Audit Log

A detailed, tamper-proof record of every action taken by a student or instructor during an exam session. Includes timestamps, interactions, events, and system-level signals.

## Attempt

A single instance of a student starting and completing (or failing to complete) an exam.

## Auto-Grading

An automated evaluation process that scores responses without instructor intervention. tAhIni supports auto-grading for objective formats like MCQs and structured short answers.

## Browser Lockdown

A mode that restricts the student’s device from switching windows, opening new tabs, or accessing external content during an exam.

## Cohort

A defined group of students taking the same course, exam, or academic period. Useful for analytics and exam scheduling.

## Exam Builder

The interface used by instructors to create, edit, and publish assessments on tAhIni.

## Exam Window

The start and end time during which students are allowed to initiate and complete an exam.

## External Examiner

A third-party reviewer (usually mandated by academic regulations) who can view or grade assessments without full instructor privileges.

## Grading Pipeline

The structured workflow tAhIni uses to process, group, review, and finalize exam responses.

## Identity Verification

A process that confirms the student’s identity before they begin an exam. Methods may include ID checks, authentication tests, or behavior-based verification.

## Integrity Event

Any recorded behavior or action that may indicate cheating or a breach of exam rules. Logged automatically for instructor review.

## Item Bank (Question Bank)

A reusable collection of exam questions stored for future use or sharing across instructors and courses.

## Manual Grading

Instructor-led evaluation where human judgment is required to assess open-ended, essay, or complex responses.

## Response Pattern Analysis

A system-level analysis that examines timing, behavior, text composition, and other metrics to detect anomalies.

## Rubric

A set of grading rules, criteria, or point allocations defined by instructors to ensure consistent scoring.

## Submission

A completed response set (answers, uploaded files, etc.) that a student sends to tAhIni at the end of an assessment.

## Submission Inbox

A centralized dashboard where instructors view, review, and manage all student submissions for a given exam.

## Timed Assessment

An exam or quiz with a strict duration once a student begins their attempt.

## Versioning

A system that tracks changes between different iterations of exams or questions to prevent confusion and maintain consistency.

## Weighted Scoring

A grading method where different questions or sections contribute different percentages to the final score.
